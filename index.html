Global Volcanic Eruption

Volcanoes are natural wonders that have fascinated and sometimes terrified us throughout history. Since the early days, volcanic eruptions have been happening frequently everywhere around the world. These natural events can vary greatly in size and impact, from small, relatively harmless outbursts to colossal explosions that reshape landscapes and impact global climate.

Understanding the patterns and behaviors of volcanic eruptions is crucial for the safety of communities living near active volcanoes. It also helps scientists, researchers and the community anticipate and mitigate the potential hazards associated with these events.

In this visualisation, we will dive into the world of volcanoes and look at the different eruptions which happened around the world thoughout the years. We will also learn about their distribution and characteristics of this natural phenomenon.

Area Chart Of Volcanic Explosivity Index

What is VEI?

The Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) is a measure to quantify and categorize the explosiveness and magnitude of volcanic eruptions on a scale from 0 to 8 . A VEI of 0 corresponds to non-explosive eruptions, often characterized by the effusion of lava, while a VEI of 8 represents the most cataclysmic and devastating volcanic eruptions, with the potential to have global climatic effects.

What does the chart tells us?

The area chart shows that the VEI which has the highest number of eruptions is 2 . Most volcanic eruptions either have a VEI of 2 or 3 . Only few volcanic eruptions have extreme VEI (0 or 8).

Bar Chart Of Damages Caused By Volcanic Eruptions

Types Of Damages

Volcanic eruptions can cause a range of devastating damages, including loss of life, injuries, destruction of homes, and people going missing. Hot lava flows, pyroclastic flows, and volcanic gases can lead to fatalities. Additionally, eruptions can cause injuries to those who are exposed to ashfall, projectiles, or scalding gases. Volcanic eruptions also result in the destruction of houses and infrastructure, including roads and utilities, due to the sheer force of the eruptive events and the accumulation of volcanic materials like ash and pumice. People may go missing due to the chaos and disarray caused by eruptions, making it challenging for authorities to account for everyone's safety.
